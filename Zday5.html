I started the CSS code for the blog and designed it, and I also added some elements to the HTML files. I finished everything around the afternoon. I showed the result to my partner and she approved of my design. With that settled, we started to edit the whole website and add our information and blog entries.

In the evening, we started using Git Bash to commit our progress and push our work to GitHub. This was a bit of a learning curve for me, and I struggled quite a bit at first. I ran into an error where I could not push my files to the repository, and in an attempt to fix it, I used a force push. Unfortunately, that caused my partner's commits to disappear from the history, but thankfully, she was able to resolve the issue and recover the work.

I think I had attempted to pull the files earlier, but I was not entirely sure how to bring the changes back into my local environment. This experience was a huge lesson for me. I now realize that to collaborate effectively on GitHub, I must always pull the latest changes made by my partner before I try to push my own. It was a stressful moment, but it definitely made me more careful with my Git commands.
