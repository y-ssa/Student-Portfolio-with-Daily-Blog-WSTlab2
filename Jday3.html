Tuesday: Campus Wins & Long Hours

Today is Tuesday - a school day. I woke up early at 6:30am because I had class. No slow morning today. I went straight into my routine, ate breakfast, and prepared my things for school. By 9;00am, I left home to start my journey for my 10:00am class. Thankfully, there was no traffic, so the ride was smooth and dtress-free. Small wins like that already set a good mood for the day.

I arrived about 15 minutes early, which gave me time to review a little for an upcoming quiz. A few minutes later, class officially started - and the best part? I found out I got a high score on our first quiz last week in that subject. That moment alone made waking up early worth it.

After class, my friends and I ate lunch at the CS canteen. Nothing beats campus lunch with friends - simple food, loud conversations, and shared stress about academics. Later, we headed to the library to review and wait for our professor beacuse we were expecting a quiz. We stayed there for hours... reviewing, talking quietly, and mentally preparing ourselves. But plot twist: the quiz was cancelled. All that nervous ebnergy for nothing - but at least we were prepared.

We then went back to CS to pick a capstone project from previous graduate batches for our activity. Seeing past projects feels motivating - like a glimpse of what we'll eventually accomplish too. After that, I decided to go home already. I ate dinner, then stepped out again to work on some school tasks. The grind doesn't stop.

By 8:30pm, I finally went back home to rest. It was a long day - early wake-up, campus hours, library waiting, cancelled quiz, schoolwork at night. But today reminded me of something important: progress doesn't always look load. Sometimes it looks like showing up, reviewing even when the quiz gets cancelled, celebrating small academnic wins, and still pushing through until night.

Tuesday was tiring - but fulfilling.
